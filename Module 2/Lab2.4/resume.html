      Use centrifugation and filtration techniques to concentrate cells for cytological analysis.
  • Staining and Slide Preparation:
      Perform routine staining, such as Papanicolaou (Pap) staining, for the identification of abnormal cellular structures.
      Carry out special staining techniques when required for the diagnosis of malignancies, infections, or inflammatory diseases.
      Ensure high-quality slide preparation for optimal microscopic analysis.
  • Microscopic Analysis and Screening:
      Examine stained cytology slides under a microscope to identify cellular abnormalities, such as cancerous, precancerous, or infectious cells.
      Assist cytopathologists in screening and diagnosing conditions based on cellular morphology.
      Report suspicious or abnormal findings to senior cytotechnologists or cytopathologists for further evaluation.
  • Record Management and Reporting:
      Accurately document patient information, test results, and observations in laboratory information systems.
      Prepare detailed reports on cytological findings and ensure timely submission to pathologists or clinicians.
      Maintain records of cytology samples and ensure proper documentation of test results.
  • Laboratory Safety and Quality Control:
      Follow established protocols for handling and processing cytological specimens to ensure specimen integrity and diagnostic accuracy.
      Adhere to laboratory safety guidelines, including the proper disposal of biohazardous materials.
      Participate in quality control programs to monitor the consistency and reliability of cytology testing procedures.
  • Equipment Maintenance and Calibration:
      Operate and maintain laboratory equipment such as centrifuges, microscopes, and staining machines.
      Ensure routine calibration of equipment to avoid test discrepancies and maintain high-quality diagnostic services.
  • Collaboration and Communication:
      Collaborate with pathologists, cytotechnologists, and laboratory staff to ensure the smooth operation of diagnostic procedures.
      Communicate effectively with healthcare professionals to provide accurate and timely results for patient care.

Histology - Medical Laboratory Technician

Anatomical Pathology | Christchurch | June 2023 - Current

  • Tissue Preparation and Sectioning:
      Perform gross examination and dissection of tissue samples under supervision.
      Prepare and embed tissue specimens in paraffin blocks to preserve samples for microscopic examination.
      Operate microtomes to cut thin tissue sections for mounting on slides.
  • Staining Techniques:
      Perform routine and special staining techniques, such as Hematoxylin and Eosin (H&E) staining, to highlight tissue structures.
      Carry out histochemical and immunohistochemical staining procedures for diagnostic purposes. Ensure optimal staining quality for clear visualization of cellular and tissue components.
  • Microscopy and Analysis:
      Utilize microscopes to examine stained tissue sections for abnormalities and provide assistance to pathologists during diagnosis.
      Identify and document histological features relevant to specific medical conditions.
      Record and report results of the analysis in accordance with established protocols.
  • Equipment Maintenance and Calibration:
      Maintain and calibrate laboratory equipment, such as microtomes, tissue processors, and staining machines.
      Ensure that all equipment is functioning properly to avoid disruption in diagnostic workflows.
  • Laboratory Safety and Quality Control:
      Follow standard operating procedures (SOPs) and safety protocols to maintain a safe working environment.
      Participate in quality control and assurance programs to ensure the accuracy and precision of histological techniques.
      Handle and dispose of biohazardous materials in compliance with laboratory safety standards.
  • Documentation and Record-Keeping:
      Maintain accurate records of tissue samples, staining procedures, and test results.
      Update laboratory information systems with patient and test data to support timely and accurate reporting.
      Prepare detailed reports of histological findings for pathologists and clinicians.
